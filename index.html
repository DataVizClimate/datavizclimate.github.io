Global Temperature Rise

As society is continuing into the future, the temperatures are starting to change between years. Where the temperatures are rising; leading to summers becoming hotter and winters becoming warmer. The main cause of this phenomenon is GreenHouse gas emissions that affect the earth’s atmosphere in being more susceptible to the sun, due to the weakening of the earth’s layer that lessens the strength of the Sun’s rays.

The countries that are leaders in the emissions of these gasses are China, United States, India and the European Union. From 1743 the global temperatures have risen by 17.14 percent with the highest temperature being 38.234 ℃ in Kuwait in 2013 from the average of 24.19 ℃ in 1850.

Sea Level Rise

Due to the increasing temperature, this has led for the colder areas to be warmer and furthermore, lead to sea level rise. The effects of sea level rise can be felt most by the island nations/ states, where sea levels are rising and thus leading for the tides to rise as high tides encroach more into the islands, into society, and into homes. This has led to increasing flooding over the years, and affects those living in the coastal regions the most.


Resolution

Despite the rising temperatures, and sea level rise, there are efforts to mitigate these ongoing issues. These efforts include reducing carbon dioxide emissions along with converting energy resources from fossil fuels to renewable energy. In 2021, the United States was readmitted to the Paris Agreement, known as an international treaty on climate change, where nations from around the world seek to mitigate the effects of climate change.

The state of Hawaii aims to have 100% of its energy from renewable resources by 2045. As of 2022, Hawaii has 29% of all its energy from renewable sources. This goal may seem ambitious, however there are several countries that are already leading the charge in 100% of their electricity generated by renewable sources such as Albania, Iceland, and Paraguay.

We are the next generation, and we are looking to ensure a renewable future. The future and horizons are bright!
